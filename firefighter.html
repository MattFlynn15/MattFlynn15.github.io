Volunteer Firefighter Shortage

This scrolly map depicts the volunteer fire fighter shortage using a three-act narrative. Volunteer fire departments across the nation are faced with decreasing fire fighters and this is particularly dangerous for rural communities who rely heavily on volunteers. First, I found all of my datasets from official sources including the National Fire Protection Association, the National Volunteer Fire Council, and the United States Fire Administration. I used excel to create the graphs and ArcGIS Desktop to create the static map. I then further designed the graphs and the multi-variate map in Adobe Illustrator - I used a minimalist approach using only red, blue, and grayscale colors and I reduced as much noise as possible by only including what was absolutely necessary. Finally, I designed the final framework using Story Maps. I used the side-car to enhance the linear flow of the story while giving incremental chunks of information in each slide. I sparsely used red and blue text as a visual accenting and redundancy technique – the red refers to volunteer while the blue refers to career. I used a dark theme with a black background and red accenting to provoke an unpleasant activation as my goal was to portray the dangers of this trend. I aspire to become a volunteer firefighter which inspired me to tell a story about the position they are currently facing. Volunteer fire departments are a vital component to our Nation’s emergency response infrastructure, so I hope this story sheds some light on what is at stake.
