Mapbox Tileset

- This slippy tileset depicts the aesthetic style of a golf course. I am a recreational golfer, and this inspired me to deconstruct the artwork associated with a golf course – the website, score card, and course map. I first took screen shots of these pieces of art and then deconstructed the aesthetic style into its form, colors, type, and textures. The most obvious aesthetic of the map is the color – various shades of greens are used as the base color to mimic the rough, the fairway, and the putting green; tan was used for green spaces to mimic the sand traps; white was used for the road network which mimics the pathways; and the black icons portray the thick black type that is used for the scorecard. I created icons that represented various sports locations. I designed these vector graphics in adobe illustrator mainly using the shapes tools and then converting them to an outline. One thing that I kept in mind throughout the entire design process was to keep the complexity relatively low. I never thought I would be able to combine my love for golf with my cartographic work, but this project shows that anything can be deconstructed to its core constituents and re-visualized on another platform.
