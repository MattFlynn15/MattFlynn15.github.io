About Me

Hello, I'm Matt!

I am a senior at UW-Madison studying Geography, Cartography, and Geographic Information Systems with a certificate in Environmental Studies.

Civic engagement has long been and will continue to be a top priority in my life. Since coming to UW-Madison in 2017 I have been heavily involved on and off campus from working as a peer advisor for Cross College Advising Services and the Morgridge Center for Public Service to volunteering with Badger Volunteers and the local Habitat for Humanity chapter. I have also remained involved with my hometown church in Brookfield, Wisconsin where I have volunteered at local food pantries, homeless shelters, drug and alcohol rehabilitation centers, as well as mission trips to New York City after Hurricane Sandy helping with disaster relief and to El Salvador focussing on community-building.

Outside of this I am an outdoors enthusiast! It's hard for me to pick a favorite season because each one provides an opportunity to enjoy the outdoors in a unique way. Once the snow starts to melt it is likely that I am starting to organize and prepare all of my fishing gear for the Spring and Summer; come Summer and into the Fall, you will likely find me fishing, hiking, kayaking, biking, and golfing up in Sayner, Wisconsin at my family's cabin; finally, in the Winter I love to go skiing at local hills in Wisconsin and in the Upper Peninsual of Michigan.
